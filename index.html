¿Qué es Smoke & Code?

A room full of people
building the future

Smoke & Code is a community meetup for anyone in the RGV who wants to build with AI — no computer-science degree required. We meet in person at Cigar Society, open our laptops, and make something real together. Grab a cigar from the lounge, light up, and code.

No curriculum, no commitment. We're enthusiasts who wanted a place to build in person — cigars, code, and good company. Come for the full afternoon or drop in for a bit. The lounge is open either way. Bienvenidos todos.

Hands-on, not a lecture

You code along the whole time. We learn by shipping, not by watching slides.

Built for the Valley

Run by locals, for locals. Bilingual help is always in the room — pregunta sin pena.

Leave with something real

Walk out with a working project and the confidence to keep going on your own.

Cómo funciona · The format

Three parts, one afternoon

Someone kicks things off with an idea. Then you code, you smoke, you build. At the end, the mic opens — show what you made, or just watch and steal ideas. No pressure either way.

Lesson 01~30 min

The Spark

Someone shares an idea, a technique, or a live demo. Tight 30-minute talk — no slides required, just real code and real thinking to get the room going.

Lesson 02~2 hr

Build & Burn

Two hours of hands-on coding with a cigar in hand. Work solo or pair up. Chase the idea you just heard, scratch your own itch, or get unstuck with help from the room.

Part 03~30 min

Show & Ship

The mic is open. Demo what you built — no judgment, no pressure. Ship it if you're ready, or kick back and see what everyone else made. Totally optional.

Arrive when you like, stay as long as you like. We're enthusiasts who wanted a place to build together.

Q.Is it really free?
Yes — completely. No fees, no catch. Food and drinks are on us too.
Q.Do I need to know how to code?
Nope. Many people come with zero experience. That's exactly who these sessions are built for.
Q.¿Se habla español?
Sí. Sessions run in English with bilingual help in the room — ask questions in whichever language you're comfortable with.
Q.What should I bring?
A laptop and a charger. We'll help you set up everything else when you arrive.
Q.Can I bring a friend?
Please do. Just have them RSVP too so we can save enough seats and snacks.
Q.Can I come late or leave early?
Of course. We're here for the afternoon — join when it suits you.
